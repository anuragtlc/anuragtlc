Teaching Learning Practices

Think Pair Share

Think Pair Share
The learning activity involves explaining answers/ideas to another student.The instrutor poses a question to the class. Students write a response and then share it with a student nearby. Students clarify their positions and discuss points of agreement and disagreement. The instructor can use several answers to illustrate important points or facilitate a whole class dicussion.
1. Instructor poses question to class
2. Students write a response (1-2 minutes)
3. Students pair up with another student nearby
4. Each student explains his/her response to the other.
5. If they disagree, each clarifies his/her position and determine how/why they disagree.

Group Problem Solving

Group Based Learning
Group Based Learning activity involve students working together to solve a problem. Activity involve:
1. Presenting students with a problem.
2. Providing some structure or guidance toward solving the problem. Note, however that they are all student-centered activities in which the instructor may have a very minimal role.
3. Reaching a final outcome or solution

JigSaw

JigSaw
Jigsaw Activity Involves:
1.Home Groups consisting of 5 members in a team are created.
2.Set of questions displayed to all the groups.
3. Each member in a group will take up a question from all the set of questions
4. The home group members will be preparing their answers individually
6. Then all the members from each group who received similar questions will be formed as master groups
7. Then the members of the master group will be getting expertize on the question that they received
8. Later the master group will be dissolved and all the members moved back to the home groups.
9. Then the faculty will be asking a question and getting responses from each group as to award marks. This will be continued for all the questions .

Project Based Learning

Project Based Learning
In Project Based Learning (PBL) activity students learn by actively engaging in real-world and personally meaningful projects.Project Based Learning is a teaching method in which students gain knowledge and skills by working for an extended period of time to investigate and respond to an authentic, engaging, and complex question, problem, or challenge.

Class Room Response Systems

Kahoot
Kahoot! is a game-based learning platform, used as educational technology in many educational institutions. Its learning games, "Kahoots", are multiple-choice quizzes that allow user generation and can be accessed via a web browser or the Kahoot app. Kahoot! can be used to review students' knowledge, for formative assessment, or as a break from traditional classroom activities.
Kahoot Activity involves:
1. Preparation of questionaire and setting up of options.
2. Students can access Kahoot.it and after keying game pin quiz will be launched.
3. Students can be seen on the projected screen and options can be seen from their devices.
4. At the end top 3 scorers will be displayed.

Plickers
One way to encourage student engagement is by using electronic devices that allow students to record their answers to multiple choice questions & allow you to instantly display the results. The anonymity encourages participation, and their answers help the teacher know when further discussion is needed. Use of Plickers can serve as a catalyst for discussion.
Plickers Activity involves:
1. Downloading of plicker cards from Plickers.com based on the number of participants.
2. Preparation of questionaire and setting up of options.
3. Faculty has to logged into the plickers app.
4. Questions were displayed to the students and the students need to answer using the plicker cards.
5.Students answers were captured using the plickers app and the correct answer can be revealed once the sharing process is completed.
